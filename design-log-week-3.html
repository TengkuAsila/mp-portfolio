New repository and editing


Posted by Tengku Asila on 25 November 2019

Web design

We had to create a new repository called mp-portfolio and copy all the data from the previous repository onto the new one. After committing the changes onto GitHub, Christine taught us about the importance of colour theory for web design. She explained the different terms used to explain the choice of colour palette such as complementary colours which is when there are two colours that are on the opposite sides of the colour wheel. Next are monochromatic colours which are three shades, tones and tints of one base colour and lastly there are analogous colours which are three colours that are side by side on the colour wheel. She also mentioned the difference regarding shades, tints and tones. According to Canva (2019), a shade is created when black is added to a base hue and making the colour darker. Whereas a tint is when white is added to a base hue thus creating a lighter colour. Lastly, a tone is created by combining both black and white with a base hue. Next, we touched on the topic about the psychology of colour where we discussed about how a specific colour is associated to a mood or the psychological meaning behind the colour. This method is beneficial for my work as a repository is the base of my website.

Photography

For photography, Lawrence touched on audio production and editing. He mentioned about the importance of creating a checklist when it comes to audio recording or even in photography and videography. We were then asked to work in the groups that we were assigned for the assignment and were asked to set up the equipment as well as record a practice interview. The basics that were touched in the lesson will be very helpful for my audio content because I will be able to know the basics when it comes to recording audio. After the lesson, I found out the importance of sound check, the recording location and setting up the equipment properly. This is really helpful when it comes to producing my audio content.


Accessibility and inclusivity


Posted by Tengku Asila on 26 November 2019

Web design

Today, we were talking about accessibility and inclusivity in web design. This topic talks about how the website should be accessible to all regardless the platform the choose and the design of the website has to draw on the full range of human diversity. We were then divided into groups and was asked to look at some examples of bad website designs. We were also asked to critique the design and give suggestions on how to change the website to make it look more inclusive and accessible. Christine also talked about the principles of inclusive design such as prioritising the content, considering the situation, be consistent, give control to the user to interact with the content in their preferred way, offer choices, provide a comparable experience for all users and lastly, consider the value of the features and how they improve the experience for different users. Next, we were taught on how to create alt text to describe an image. We were also told to use colours with sufficient contrast, ensure the language is simple and easy to read, the type sizes are not too big or small and the mobile touch targets should be 44px by 44px as a minimum. This lesson talks about the importance of making sure your website is accessible and inclusive for every user. This is beneficial when it comes to designing my website because I have to always make sure my website is accessible to all users. Even the smallest details may be a big issue for a certain user. By considering all the aspects that were said, it will help ensure my website is inclusive and accessible by all.

Audio

The first thing that we had to do is to edit the practice interview we did the day before on Audition. This is to ensure that we know the basics such as importing the file onto Audition and playing around with the tools to come up with a final edited interview. We were also reminded about the importance of backing up your work properly. Next, we discussed about the do’s and do not’s when it comes to interviewing. First thing to always remember is to always do research regarding the person you are interviewing to ensure that you do not get any wrong facts about the person. Make sure you structure your interview well. Come up with a proper script and write down your answers prior to the interview. Another important note is to make sure you know your audience. You should not make racial comments when interviewing somebody. After class, my group and I went into town to take more photographs for our portfolio. By doing a practice run, this will help ensure my team and I on what to do and not to do when interviewing somebody. Besides that, this lesson has also showed us the importance of backing up all our work and to organise our files accordingly to make the editing process easier.
