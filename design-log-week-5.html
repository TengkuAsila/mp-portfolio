Editing and uploading images


Posted by Tengku Asila on 9 December 2019

Web design

For class, we were allowed to break out and work on our website. I chose to upload some of the pictures I have taken during the last photography session as well as uploading the audio interview onto Soundcloud and embedding it onto Atom. After resizing the image on Photoshop, I saved the photos into the portfolio folder under the website folder. I then uploaded the photos onto Atom and saved it. After doing that, I committed it onto GitHub. This method has been proven to work when it comes to uploading images onto the website. After doing the method, it actually made uploading images easier than expected.

Photography

For the photography session, we were also allowed to break out and work on our website. I decide to edit the photos that I have taken onto Photoshop by increasing the exposure and contrast on some of the images as well as cropping the image to the make it more proportional. My team and I also edited the audio interview on Adobe Audition. After class, we went out into town and took a few more photos to make the website more complete. After knowing the basics of editing on photoshop, I am able to edit my photos to make it nicer just by increasing a few details. The method that were taught on using Adobe Audition helped us in editing our audio interview for the website.

Web design

Videography

Lawrence brought in a guest lecture, Kevin Palmer, who is a renowned sports journalist. He talked about the journalism world and shared some tips on how to get someone to agree to be interviewed. He also mentioned about how it is important to not be afraid to try new things and to chase your desired job roles. The topics discussed in the lecture can be used in my work especially regarding the tips on interviewing. Coming from someone in the industry, it is really insightful to see the methods he used to get an interview from someone. Some of the methods will be applicable to when we have to interview our chosen guest for the assignment.


Digital Wednesday


Posted by Tengku Asila on 11 December 2019

Web design

For digital Wednesday, we were asked to watch a 20 minutes video regarding the importance of inclusive and accessible technology. The speaker, Molly Watt discusses about the importance of making sure your product whether it be a website or an application to be accessible and inclusive. A few important things she mentioned was when designing, you should always be aware of what this product is doing and working with it to oppose of working against it. Another fascinating point she made was that designers and developers to be aware of the grey areas. Everyone is in the grey area. She mentioned how accessibility and inclusivity does not just focus on the minorities, but it covers all (Watt, 2018). Disabilities can either be temporary, situational or permanent. Similar to what Christine has mentioned in the session regarding inclusivity and accessibility. After watching the video, I am now able to understand more regarding the issue and how important it is to make everyone feel included. With that being said, I will know be able to reflect on my website and see if my website is inclusive and accessible enough.

Videography

My team and I went to National Taekwondo Centre to have an interview with Bianca Walkden. After the interview, we edited the interview on Adobe Premier Pro. For the interview, we used the methods taught to us such as the 180-degree angle. For the editing, we used the Premier Pro software and used the techniques taught in class to produce the final video.
